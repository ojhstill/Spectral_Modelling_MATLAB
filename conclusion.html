CONCLUSION

Although relatively simple in its capabilities, the system provides a comprehensive approach to spectral modelling synthesis and audio transformation. With only minimal input controls, users can adjust the timbral and tonal qualities of any audio sample in experimental ways to ultimately hear and view the spectral effects. However, the system transformation relies on the external MATLAB package ‘SMSMatlab’ and thus is not completely transparent to a general user.

Additionally, a feature to improve is the pitch transposition as the deterministic transformations rely on mathematical relationships between frequencies, known as equal temperament, rather than the more conventional 'just intonation' tuning standard. Additionally, the resonance balance's sensitivity needs adjusting as the effects are only noticeable for extreme range values. Through the system design breakdown and audio examples, the hope is that this website article has given an understanding of spectral model synthesis and how it can implemented in an audio based system.
